About

Background

I am a doctoral researcher affiliated with INPE (Instituto Nacional de Pesquisas Espaciais), currently based in London. My work sits at the intersection of solar physics, machine learning, and computational magnetohydrodynamics.

My primary case study focuses on NOAA Active Region 3663 around an X-class flare event in May 2024. I develop and apply multi-channel DEM inversion pipelines on SDO/AIA data to reconstruct temperature and density maps of the corona, and validate nonlinear force-free field (NLFFF) extrapolations produced by a physics-informed neural network framework (PyMHS) against established numerical methods.

Research

Current Projects

01

Coronal DEM Analysis

Pixel-by-pixel differential emission measure inversions of 6-channel SDO/AIA EUV data to reconstruct temperature and electron density maps of NOAA AR3663 across pre-flare and flare phases.

02

NLFFF Extrapolation via PINNs

Developing PyMHS — a physics-informed neural network for nonlinear force-free magnetic field extrapolation — and benchmarking it against CCMC/NASA numerical reference solutions.

03

Coronal Heating Diagnostics

Applying RTV scaling laws along NLFFF field lines with DEM-derived thermodynamic parameters to estimate volumetric heating rates and coronal energy flux distributions.

04

Flare Event Characterization

Multi-wavelength study of the May 2024 X-class flare in AR3663, characterizing plasma dynamics, magnetic topology changes, and energy release mechanisms.
